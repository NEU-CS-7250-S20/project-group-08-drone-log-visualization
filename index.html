Project Team 8: Drone Log Visualization, CS 7250 S20

Hai Nguyen and Ondrej Biza

Service-Learning Course Project as part of CS 7250 S20: Information Visualization: Theory and Applications, taught by Prof. Cody Dunne, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Motivation

Autonomous unmanned aerial vehicles are sophisticated systems with hardware, software, and humans working together. For this reason, during research and development, it is crucial to have a visualization tool that can help verify system correctness, identify possible errors or anomalies of the system for improvements. Some visualizations already exist but often lack a visualization-based approach. Often these are inefficient and to have a comprehensive analysis of the system, users might have to switch between tabs or to use additional tools. This study helps to address these problems by introducing a useful visualization for post-flight analysis of drone logs. The tool allows users both to see the big picture by displaying all essential data at once. The tool can autonomously analyze the log to find pre-defined errors. Then the tool lets users select areas of interest to look further by examining other data sources displayed in synchronized charts.
